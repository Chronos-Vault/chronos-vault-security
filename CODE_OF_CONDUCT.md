# Code of Conduct

**Trinity Protocol™ Security Community**  
Version 2.0 | December 2025

---

## Our Commitment

Trinity Protocol is committed to fostering a safe, respectful, and collaborative security research community. We believe that **ethical security research** makes blockchain technology safer for everyone.

**"Trust Math, Not Humans"**  Our security philosophy extends to our community: we rely on transparent processes and mathematical proofs, not blind trust.

---

## Expected Behavior

### Research & Testing
- **Test on testnets only** (Arbitrum Sepolia, Solana Devnet, TON Testnet)
- **Report privately** to chronosvault@chronosvault.org
- **Provide clear details** (reproduction steps, impact analysis)
- **Collaborate respectfully** with our security team
- **Wait for fixes** before public disclosure (90 days)

### Communication
- Be professional in all interactions
- Respect timelines for responses and fixes
- Ask questions if anything is unclear
- Share constructive feedback
- Acknowledge prior work by other researchers

### Ethics
- **Protect user data** - Never access, modify, or share
- **Minimize impact** - Don't degrade service availability
- **Follow disclosure** guidelines responsibly
- **Respect scope** - Stay within bug bounty boundaries

---

## Prohibited Actions

- Testing on mainnet with real user funds
- Accessing user data or private information
- Extortion or threats against the project
- Public disclosure before coordinated fix
- Social engineering attacks on team members
- DDoS or service disruption attacks

---

## Enforcement

Violations of this code of conduct may result in:
1. Warning and guidance
2. Temporary suspension from bug bounty program
3. Permanent ban from community participation
4. Legal action for malicious activities

---

## Reporting

Report code of conduct violations to: chronosvault@chronosvault.org

All reports are reviewed confidentially.
